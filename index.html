Our Philosophy

We size risk to the regime — we don't bet on direction.

RegimeTrader is built on one core belief: most of what looks like skill in markets is actually just exposure to volatility at the wrong time. Rather than forecasting price direction, our engine continuously classifies the market into a small set of statistical regimes — defined by realized volatility and drift — using a Gaussian Hidden Markov Model fit on rolling return data.

The model selects between 3 and 7 latent states using BIC, so complexity is earned by the data, not assumed in advance. Each regime carries its own volatility-based target exposure: calm, trending markets receive full allocation, while turbulent markets are sized down automatically — long before a drawdown becomes painful.

We deliberately run an always-long book. Shorting introduces asymmetric tail risk and borrow costs that, in our research, are rarely compensated by the marginal edge of a regime model. Defense, in our system, means holding less — not betting against the market.

σ
Volatility-Based Sizing Position size is a direct function of the detected regime's realized volatility — not a fixed leverage target. Low-Vol/BULL regimes run near full exposure; High-Vol regimes are scaled down mechanically.
∅
No Shorting, Ever The strategy never holds a net-short position. In defensive regimes, capital rotates toward cash or low-volatility allocation rather than betting on further declines.
BIC
Data-Driven Regime Count The number of hidden states (3–7) is chosen by Bayesian Information Criterion on each re-fit, balancing model fit against overfitting risk.
⛔
Independent Circuit Breakers Daily drawdown, peak drawdown, volatility spikes, and broker connectivity are each monitored by an isolated breaker — any one of them can halt trading independently of the others.
